Triplets

Model Triplets

Definitions

$$Agents = \{A,B,C\} = \mathbb{A}$$ $$sets = \{X, Y\} = \mathbb{S}$$ $$Three cards per set = \{1,2,3\} = \mathbb{N}$$ $$Cards = \mathbb{S} * \mathbb{N} = \{X1,X2,X3,Y1,Y2,Y3\} = \mathbb{C}$$ $$Propositional atoms= A * C = \{A_{X1},A_{X2},A_{X3}, etc\} = \mathbb{P} $$

Notation

The following notations will be used in this paper to model asking for and receiving cards.
Agent a asks agent b for card x: $$[?abx]$$ For the case that a player gives a card to another player, we will use substitutions as defined by van Benthem et al.
Agent a gives card x to agent b: $$a_x \mapsto b_x$$

Knowledge Before the Start of the Game

Everyone starts with exactly two cards: $$ \forall i \in \mathbb{A}: (i_{x1} \land i_{x2} \land \neg(i_{x3}) \land \neg(i_{y1}) \land \neg(i_{y2}) \land \neg(i_{y3})) \lor \\ ((i_{x1} \land i_{x3} \land \neg(i_{x2}) \land \neg(i_{y1}) \land \neg(i_{y2}) \land \neg(i_{y3})) \lor \\ ((i_{x1} \land i_{y1} \land \neg(i_{x2}) \land \neg(i_{x3}) \land \neg(i_{y2}) \land \neg(i_{y3})) \lor \\ ... \lor \\ ((i_{y2} \land i_{y3} \land \neg(i_{x1}) \land \neg(i_{x2}) \land \neg(i_{x3}) \land \neg(i_{y1})) $$

Rules During the Game

Each card is hold by exactly one player $$ \forall c \in \mathbb{C}: (A_{c} \Rightarrow \neg B_{c} \land \neg C_{c}) \land (B_{c} \Rightarrow \neg A_{c} \land \neg C_{c}) \land (C_{c} \Rightarrow \neg A_{c} \land \neg B_{c}) $$ If a player has a set complete, then he will always announce this $$ \forall i \in \mathbb{A}, \forall s \in \mathbb{S}: (i_{s1} \land i_{s2} \land i_{s3}) \Rightarrow [i_{s1}] \land [i_{s2}] \land [i_{s3}] $$ A player can only ask for a card if the player has a card of the set in his hand $$ \forall i, j \in A, \forall s \in C, \forall n \in N : [?isnj] \Rightarrow j_{s1} \lor j_{s2} \lor j_{s3} $$ A player only asks for cards that he does not hold himself $$ \forall i,j \in A, \forall c \in C: \neg([?icj]\land i_{c}) $$ If you are asked for a card that you hold in your hand, then you have to hand this card over $$ \forall i,j \in A, \forall c \in C: (([?icj]\land j_{c}) \mapsto (i_c \land \neg{j_c})) $$ If a player gives a card to another player, then the first player does not hold the card anymore and the second player has the card $$ \forall i,j \in A, \forall c \in C: [!ijc] \Rightarrow j_{c} \land \neg{i_{c}} $$
